About

I am a 4th year CSE undegrad student at UIET, Panjab University, Chandigarh, India and also a part of the research team at Openmined.
My current research interest include Natural Language Processing, Privacy and Fairness in machine learning problems pertaining to differential privacy and underrepresentation of minority groups by large models like BERT.
Previously, I've worked on glaucoma diagnostics using deep learning techniques with Dr. Mamta Juneja and Dr. Prashant Jindal.
I am open to collaborations, so feel free to email me if you have a cool idea and want to discuss it.

Projects

Privacy Attacks On Masked Language Models (Ongoing)

The research project focuses on masked language models and how they react when subjected to privacy attacks.
We are aware that seq2seq models are able to memorise the contents of the training samples and use them to produce sequences, but that's not the case with BERT-based models.
BERT is mostly used for classification tasks and it is difficult to extract samples from it wihout using specific techniques like probing, providing partial information and so on.
I'm working along with Fatemehsadat Mireshghallah on this project.

Private_ClassImbalance

The project is the implementation of our paper DP-SGD vs PATE: Which Has Less Disparate Impact on Model Accuracy?
The project aims to compares the disparate impact of differential privacy mechanisms: DP-SGD and PATE, on underrepresented groups.

Differential-Privacy-Image-Captioning

The project aims to create an image captioning model which can then further be incorporated in an image search engine.
The unique feature of this project is that it incorporates the concept of differential privacy to the image captioning project, which helps us prevent any leakage of sensitive data to the public.

Indian-Sign-Language

The project aims to create an application using transfer learning to efficiently classify the Indian sign language alphabets. The classified alphabets are then combined orderly and converted into sentences.
The additional features in the project are: conversion of text to speech using Google's text to speech API and converting a video into a sequence of sentences.

DC-Gnet

The research project mainly focuses on the detection of glaucoma in the retinal fundus images through the means of Cup to Disc Ratio(CDR), Disc Damage Likelihood Scale(DDLS) and the ISNT rule.
The basic idea behind this research project was to efficiently segment the optic disc and optic cup in a retinal fundus image. The segmented disc and cup are then used to calculate the two values: CDR and DDLS, and verify the ISNT rule.

GC-Net

The research project mainly focuses on the efficient classification of a glaucomatous eye and a normal eye.
The basic idea behind this research project was to efficiently reduce the size of the existing state-of-the-art classification models and creating a custom architecture that performed better and was easily deployable.
